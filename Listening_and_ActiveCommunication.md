# Listening and Active Communication
## Question 1
### What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)

- Avoid getting distracted by your own thoughts  
- Focus on the speaker and the topic instead  
- Try not to interrupt the person  
- Let others finish before responding  
- Use door openers  
- Show that you are listening through body language  
- Take notes during important conversations  


## Question 2
### According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)

Reflective listening means listening carefully to the other person without interrupting. It is about understanding not only what they say but also how they feel. After listening, we explain their idea again in our own words to check if we understood it correctly. We can also ask questions if something is not clear. This helps the speaker feel understood and shows empathy.


## Question 3
### What are the obstacles in your listening process?

There are a few obstacles in my listening process. Sometimes I get distracted by my surroundings or my own thoughts, which makes it harder to focus. At times, I may form assumptions before the speaker finishes. I also occasionally think about my response instead of fully listening. When the topic is complex or unfamiliar, I may miss some details. I am aware of these challenges and I am working to improve my listening skills.


## Question 4
### What can you do to improve your listening?

To improve my listening, I need to focus fully on the speaker and avoid distractions. I should listen patiently without interrupting and give my full attention. Taking notes can help me remember important points, especially in technical discussions. I can also rephrase what I understood to check if it is correct. Asking questions when something is not clear and showing interest in the conversation will help me become a better listener.


## Question 5
### When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?

I sometimes use passive communication when I am in situations where I don’t want to create conflict or when I feel slightly unsure about my opinion. For example, in group discussions or when speaking with seniors, I may stay quiet even if I have something to say. However, I am trying to improve by expressing my thoughts more confidently in such situations.


## Question 6
### When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?

I rarely become aggressive, but in situations where I feel stressed or when things are not going as expected, I may feel the urge to react strongly. However, I make a conscious effort to stay calm and express my thoughts clearly instead of speaking harshly. I try to focus on the message rather than reacting emotionally.


## Question 7
### When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?

Sometimes, when I am uncomfortable expressing my feelings directly, I may show passive-aggressive behavior like giving short responses or avoiding the conversation. But I am aware of this and try to communicate more openly instead of using indirect ways like sarcasm or silence.


## Question 8
### How can you make your communication assertive?

To make my communication more assertive, I try to express my thoughts clearly and confidently while also respecting others. I focus on staying calm, listening carefully, and responding in a polite and direct way. I also try to avoid being too passive or too aggressive by maintaining a balance. Practicing this regularly helps me improve my communication and express myself without hurting others.
